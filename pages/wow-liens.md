## Why Addon Disarmament?

With the advent of Midnight, Blizzard hoped to implement a major philosophy shift in the way they, and players, engaged with AddOns. Instead of being so reliant on third-party tools, especially computational AddOns like complex boss WeakAuras, Blizzard hoped to expand their own base game tools while also curtailing the ability of AddOns, particularly combat Addons, to engage with the game. In their [long blog post](https://news.blizzard.com/en-us/article/24246290/combat-philosophy-and-addon-disarmament-in-midnight) on the topic, Blizzard acknowledged what a risky move this change could be, but also described *why* AddOn disarmament felt so important, and why **now** felt like the right moment.

Often, Blizzard developers have come head-to-head with AddOn creators, particularly WeakAura authors. AddOn devs tended to outpace Blizzard’s expectations by designing solutions to complicated boss mechanics, no matter how Blizzard attempted to circumvent WeakAura answers with different design decisions. Eventually, AddOns began to move beyond simple user interface adjustments, and became mandatory in raiding groups instead of being a personal preference; even outside of Cutting Edge guilds, and even in content like Normal difficulty raids and pickup groups (PUGs), Raid Leaders were frequently requiring certain AddOns or WeakAura packs from all attendees. Constantly attempting to balance every difficulty around the inevitability of computational WeakAura answers made boss fights feel flat, and left players who didn’t want those WeakAuras feeling increasingly frustrated.

Thus, the Midnight philosophy shift was born: AddOns should no longer offer a competitive advantage in WoW combat. In other words, AddOns should be a personal aesthetic or accessibility choice, not a mandatory part of endgame content. In line with this design shift, Blizzard also began further developing their own base game user interface features. The hope was, and remains, that players could choose to play with zero AddOns and still perform.

In the run up to Midnight’s launch, Blizzard introduced their answers to AddOns: the Assisted Highlight and One-Button Rotation features, the Cooldown Manager, the Boss Warnings system, a vanilla Damage Meter, and a wide range of accessibility features.

Midnight served as an excellent deadline for these changes, permitting Blizzard’s developers to create endgame content designed to function without computational solutions from WeakAuras. At the same time, devs could also implement new vanilla versions of many popular combat AddOns that players would adjust to as they started leveling in a new expansion. Those AddOns that could not function in the AddOn disarmament game sunset their systems during the Midnight Pre-Patch, giving players further time to adjust to these drastic new changes. This extra time to play around with new Blizzard interface options also encouraged players to jump into developing their own solutions to existing gaps in the vanilla interface. And this is how a whole slew of new AddOns were born!

## Quality of Life

### [Mailbox Plus](https://www.curseforge.com/wow/addons/mailbox-plus) by *HoneyPotts*

Mailbox Plus is the spiritual successor to my previous favorite, Postal, which does not currently have a Midnight update in the works. Whether you’re sending way too much mail to your alt army or simply collecting millions of gold in auctions every day, Mailbox Plus has a feature for that! You can forward mail, open all mail of a certain type or sender, selectively open or return mail, autotrack and autofill all your alt names (even cross-server), and utilize a truly staggering number of other features. If you find yourself at the mailbox a lot, consider making your life a little easier with this AddOn.

### [AltVault](https://www.curseforge.com/wow/addons/altvault) by *Maygus11*

![AltVault](https://u-wowretail.raiderio.net/78fc/2026/03/09/mzAWLYls6mnbyzqlMWl0gydNfT/image.jpg)

If you have played WoW for some time now, you likely have far too many alts by this point, particularly with the speed-leveling options provided by the recent Remix events. Managing bank tabs, professions, and bags across multiple alts certainly feels overwhelming…enter AltVault! With this AddOn, check rested XP, gold, Great Vault progress, and more for each alt on your account; you can also combine all your alt storage into a single giant bag for easier viewing, and check your Warbound bank from the AltVault panel. DO check out your gold totals, but DO NOT peek at the accumulated /played data across your account…trust me on this one.

### [Teleport Menu](https://www.curseforge.com/wow/addons/teleport-me-nu) by *justw8*

Blizzard has been hooking us up with a number of cool new ways to get around the world, from a myriad of neat Hearthstone toys to Engineering wormholes and most recently, Hero’s Path teleports from Mythic+. It’s extremely handy to have teleports to all the dungeons, but a little less fun to go poking through your spellbook and each expansion’s popout teleport panel to find the one you’re looking for. Enter: Teleport Menu! This lightweight AddOn simply adds your available Hearthstones and teleports to the game menu for ease of access.

### [Ping Sounds](https://www.curseforge.com/wow/addons/pingsounds) by *Nnoggie*

I need boss voice lines to keep my distracted healer brain on the mechanics at hand instead of the health bars of my zug-zugging melee DPS, but not everyone plays with game sounds on. Unfortunately, the newer in-game ping system relies upon having sound effects enabled. For those of you who would rather raid to your own music and the dulcet tones of your raid leader, with no in-game sounds at all, consider Ping Sounds – mute what’s unimportant to you and hear only the pings!

![Narcissus](https://u-wowretail.raiderio.net/d97f/2025/04/23/83CCSltbVuppG8hB5RZXBREvJ2/image.jpg)

This AddOn may take some getting used to (and I won’t lie, I needed a video or two to help me get started), but it’s well worth the time investment.

### [Plumber](https://www.curseforge.com/wow/addons/plumber) by *Peterodox*

![Plumber](https://u-wowretail.raiderio.net/2dc6/2026/03/09/Qo9PLhJIuRn7Dl1qF50zPgVFCk/image.jpg)

Plumber is one of many in a new genre of QoL AddOns designed to adjust many bits of the base Blizzard UI at once. Plumber offers a shocking number of different modules to play with, and each is individually adjustable. If I listed **all** the customizable options Plumber provides, this section would be very long, but I can easily say its adjustments to the Decor Catalog are my favorite: view decor pieces from all angles, view their in-game size with a banana for scale, and best of all, easily duplicate any piece of placed decor with a single key press. Peterodox is a real MVP, as this AddOn is constantly being developed and improved with new QoL features I’d never even known I was missing until they appeared right in front of me. In the post-AddOn disarmament world, Plumber absolutely feels like a necessity to me.

### [Account Wide Interface Settings](https://www.curseforge.com/wow/addons/account-wide-ui) by *ConineSpiritwolf*

The Midnight AddOn changes brought with them a myriad of new Blizzard UI options, designed to replace some of the WeakAuras and other in-combat AddOns so many players grew accustomed to using. Account Wide Interface Settings saves hours of work by synching each character-specific Interface Setting in the Blizzard UI, ensuring that all the hard work you’ve put into your latest creation can carry over to every character you play, without requiring adjustments each time you log on an alt. Whether you’ve just fine-tuned the placement of the Talking Head module on your screen or built out the perfect new UI for yourself, Account Wide Interface Settings guarantees even a new alt will have everything saved without any need for extra fiddling in the settings.

### [Key Master](https://www.curseforge.com/wow/addons/key-master) by *Strylor*

Key Master is an excellent addition to the AddOn list for anyone who is seriously invested in Mythic+. Not only does it grant you access to detailed, real-time Mythic+ score information for your character, but it also tracks all your alternative characters’ keys, rating, and Great Vault slots. Plus, Key Master can interface with your party members to offer you information on their key times, available keys, and overall rating gains.

### [Targeted Spells](https://www.curseforge.com/wow/addons/targetedspells) by *Xepheris*

![Targeted Spells](https://u-wowretail.raiderio.net/a554/2026/03/11/UCg1MrBwNEIChGHkU96d4IL7VS/image.jpg)

With the demise of WeakAuras in Midnight *and* the loss of almost all healer kicks, the targeted spells WAs will be very sorely missed this season. Unfortunately, the default Blizzard UI is not great at communicating information about who is targeted by which spells. Xepheris to the rescue! Targeted Spells works with both third-party unit frames AND the default Blizzard frames, includes different glows per spell, and is heavily customizable. Whether you’re looking to push keys or need something to help you keep an eye on important spell targets in raid, give Targeted Spells a try!

### [MKeysTalents](https://www.curseforge.com/wow/addons/mkeystalents) by *Scoia*

It may be difficult to replicate the WeakAuras that remind us to swap into particular *talents* before starting a key, but MKeysTalents is an excellent starting point. You will need to go through a short configuration process, defining each talent loadout you want to use per Mythic+ dungeon. After the initial setup, a ready check inside any dungeon will trigger MKeys to check whether you are using the correct loadout, and warn you if not. Never risk your timer again just because of an improper talent!

If you love running keystones, consider Astral Keys for a beautifully organized way to track the Mythic+ activity going on around you.

## User Interface

### [BugGrabber](https://www.curseforge.com/wow/addons/bug-grabber) and [BugSack](https://www.curseforge.com/wow/addons/bugsack) by *Funkeh*

These two are more important than ever with so many new changes to the API. BugGrabber is the AddOn that, as its name suggests, grabs any Lua errors. On its own, BugGrabber will simply print the errors into your chat. BugSack is a requirement if you are not a developer yourself, as it will collect any errors that BugGrabber finds and throw them into a more easily-readable format. With these two installed, you can identify any major errors in your UI, as well as sending game-breaking bugs to AddOn developers in an effort to help them improve their projects. These two AddOns will not suppress Lua errors, but will instead let you know exactly what’s going wrong and where, which leads to easier troubleshooting and reporting.

## Leveling

### [Azeroth Pilot Reloaded](https://www.curseforge.com/wow/addons/azeroth-pilot-reloaded) by *Neoldric*

![Azeroth Pilot Reloaded](https://cdnassets.raider.io/uploads/articles/659/apr_final.png)

Azeroth Auto Pilot is a much-beloved legacy leveling AddOn, and Azeroth Pilot Reloaded is the spiritual successor. Offering everything from detailed quest objective notes and attached waypoint arrows to auto quest hand-ins and quest item keybinding, APR is a required part of my alt-leveling rotation. You can even pick different routes, including those developed by WoW community members and some of the speediest levelers in the business!

---

### [QuestPop!](https://www.curseforge.com/wow/addons/questpop) by *MrDraze*

Whether you would just like to add a little more flair to your questing experience, or you genuinely find it difficult to keep track of quest objectives, QuestPop! might be a great solution for you. Extremely lightweight and customizable, QuestPop! can add sound and larger, more modern pop-up messages for quest progression and completion. This is a fabulous accessibility option to add larger quest alerts to your screen, and is also a way to give questing just a *little* more pizazz.

---

### [BTWQuests](https://www.curseforge.com/wow/addons/btw-quests) by *Breeni*

![BTWQuests](https://cdnassets.raider.io/uploads/articles/659/btw_quests.png)

Breeni offers up something awesome for dedicated loremasters and completionists with this straightforward, uncomplicated quest journal that displays your current quest, any future quests in the line, and how that questline connects to others. If you’re struggling to unlock the [Haranir allied race](https://worldofwarcraft.blizzard.com/en-us/game/races/haranir-horde) or are hoping to finally finish up those [Loremaster achievements](https://www.wowhead.com/achievement=7520/the-loremaster), swing by and pick up BTWQuests to make your journey just a little easier.

---

### [Reckless Abandon](https://www.curseforge.com/wow/addons/reckless-abandon) by *fraukebetz*

Whether you’re obsessively clean on your main character, or plagued by full quest logs on your alts (or both!), Reckless Abandon is an incredible solution that allows you to instantly abandon quests directly from your quest log, *in bulk*! You can abandon quests individually, by zone, by campaign, or by expansion. You can even configure automation, which will keep your quest log clean all the time. We may all be approaching 90 on our first characters, but that doesn’t mean your alts are prepared for the Midnight leveling journey. Scrub those quest logs!

## Maps and Open World Content

### [HandyNotes](https://www.curseforge.com/wow/addons/handynotes) by *Xinhuan*

![HandyNotes](https://u-wowretail.raiderio.net/8319/2026/03/09/MTxgnHydRjhNLvdpPlCj2i9kGd/image.jpg)

*Handynotes with Kemayo’s [Midnight Treasures & Rares plugin](https://www.curseforge.com/wow/addons/handynotes-midnight-treasures)*

HandyNotes began as a basic AddOn designed to add notes to your map. The base AddOn is lightweight and extremely simple, allowing contributors to develop all manner of plugins to best suit their needs. Grab the base AddOn first, of course, but also consider the following for all your Midnight adventures:

- [Midnight](https://www.curseforge.com/wow/addons/handynotes-midnight) plugin by *Zarillion*
- [Midnight Treasures and Rares](https://www.curseforge.com/wow/addons/handynotes-midnight-treasures) plugin by *Kemayo*
- [MapNotes](https://www.curseforge.com/wow/addons/mapnotes) plugin by *BadBoyBarny*, which allows you to add tags and notes to your map, including directions and key location tags in major cities like Silvermoon!

---

### [Leatrix Plus](https://www.curseforge.com/wow/addons/leatrix-plus) and [Leatrix Maps](https://www.curseforge.com/wow/addons/leatrix-maps) by *Leatrix*

Leatrix Plus has about a million different functions all baked into one AddOn, which makes it a valuable find all on its own. Automate quests and gossip, accept summons and rezzes, sell and repair automatically, hide or resize just about any piece of text in the game, and SO MUCH MORE. However, in conjunction with Leatrix Maps, you can also reveal the map (even for characters who haven’t explored it yet), scale and position the map just the way you’d like it, and customize the map appearance exactly to your specifications.

---

### [City Guide - Silvermoon](https://www.curseforge.com/wow/addons/city-guide) by *Valash*

![City Guide - Silvermoon](https://u-wowretail.raiderio.net/e596/2026/03/09/EIQc8rvufmdIJHYVtrB2swsAuc/image.jpg)

Some people really struggle with orienting themselves in a new capital city every expansion (it’s me, I’m some people). Like so many new AddOns in this article, City Guide replaces a popular genre of WeakAuras: map overlays. This is a lightweight little overlay that is surprisingly customizable for its size and function. If you find yourself flying aimlessly in circles before finally giving up and asking a guard (😬), give City Guide a try.

---

### [Silver Dragon: Rare Scanner](https://www.curseforge.com/wow/addons/silver-dragon) by *Kemayo*

Silver Dragon has been a staple for players since 2008, and still remains my tried and true rare-hunting companion all these years later. With customizable sounds and repeat intervals, chat messages, model popups, and a history log, you’ll be all set for some rare camping, whether you’re looking for Midnight rares or returning to [the Storm Peaks](https://www.wowhead.com/zone=67/the-storm-peaks) to hunt for the [Time Lost Proto-Drake](https://www.wowhead.com/item=44168/reins-of-the-time-lost-proto-drake).

## Professions

### [CraftSim](https://www.curseforge.com/wow/addons/craftsim) by *genjuwow*

CraftSim is a brilliant, newer AddOn designed specifically for the revamped professions introduced with the release of Dragonflight. Because crafting became still more complicated in TWW, this AddOn is absolutely worth a second look. Check out the crafting simulation mode, evaluate your average profits, simulate knowledge point distribution, and queue up recipes to multicraft with a single click. CraftSim is also modular and highly-configurable, enabling you to choose only the pieces of the AddOn you find most helpful to your personal crafting endeavors.

### [Weekly Knowledge](https://www.curseforge.com/wow/addons/weeklyknowledge) by *Liquidor*

For those who have a lot of crafting alts to maintain, Weekly Knowledge may be your preferred professions knowledge tracker. This AddOn presents your profession knowledge progress on every character on your account, all laid out in a tidy grid. Keep building that alt army, and don’t forget any weekly quests!

### [Myu’s Knowledge Points Tracker](https://www.curseforge.com/wow/addons/myus-knowledge-points-tracker) by *myu_westfall*

A big thank you to Myu, who replaced their enormously popular knowledge points tracker WeakAura with this little AddOn when the Midnight changes rolled out. Track unique treasures, weekly knowledge point rewards, catch-up point currency, and remaining knowledge points needed to fill out your current tree.

### [No Mats; No Make](https://www.curseforge.com/wow/addons/nomatsnomake) by *Teelo*

Never accept an outrageously expensive order from that little cheapskate Milhouse Manastorm ever again with No Mats; No Make! This amazing AddOn makes just a few adjustments to the Crafting Orders UI, which is especially helpful when fulfilling Patron Orders. ## Housing

### [Advanced Decoration Tools](https://www.curseforge.com/wow/addons/adt) by *rofan*

If you’re a newfound housing main, like I am, Advanced Decoration Tools is almost mandatory for long building sessions. Use traditional keyboard shortcuts to duplicate, cut, copy, and paste. Drop your favorite decor objects on the handy Quickbar. Never dig through your dyes again for that perfect color scheme; the 5-slot Dye Presets function has you covered. Use the incremental rotation feature for those complicated architectural builds…and then **lock** any finished builds so you no longer pick up a tiny piece and disrupt the entire thing! ADT is a powerhouse for serious builders.

---

### [DecorDrops](https://www.curseforge.com/wow/addons/decordrops) by *ParallaxDev*

![DecorDrops](https://u-wowretail.raiderio.net/a2e8/2026/03/09/3CVQlUy1oMnFR8Np6kwYGsRS1i/image.png)

DecorDrops offers a fabulous and very familiar layout that feels a lot like the Blizzard Adventure Journal, but it also provides players with so much more. Sort decor by source or expansion, with extensive search options. Those who are successful on the decor market will love the display options, which clearly show resource, reagent, and vendor costs. DecorDrops will also work alongside Auctionator to integrate material and crafted decor prices. Waypoints and teleport buttons are also integrated right into the DecorDrops UI to make seeking out decor a much simpler task.

---

### [Decor Vendor](https://www.curseforge.com/wow/addons/decor-vendor) by *MidniteDestiny*

![Decor Vendor](https://u-wowretail.raiderio.net/4d46/2026/03/09/RAWSktGJMBDox4r5tUKBvb2xXt/image.png)

As its name suggests, Decor Vendor focuses specifically on vendor-sourced decor and items with unlock requirements. The UI clearly displays exactly who the vendor is, where they are in the world, what you’ve already discovered or purchased from their vendor table, and which quests or achievements you must still complete before you can obtain the items sold. Filter by faction or expansion, and right-click any vendor name to set a waypoint. This AddOn may not be as expansive as some of the others in the Housing section, but it is VERY helpful for tracking your quest and achievement progress on those tricky reputation-based decor unlocks!

---

### [Home Decor](https://www.curseforge.com/wow/addons/home-decor) by *Azro*

Home Decor provides a major Decor Catalog and tooltip glow-up, but that’s not all. It also offers Crafting and Shopping Lists for all decor, allowing you to track materials and prices for craftable pieces whether you’re selling or buying. Decor Auction keeps tabs on all your crafted pieces, while Sales Tracker will tally your earnings. The Endeavors tab, a new feature, spruces up the basegame tab with a clear count on the days remaining, coupon tracking, multi-neighborhood support, account-wide contribution calculations, and best of all, smart task ranking that will factor in which tasks earn you the most XP. The DecorTracker window tracks collection progress by zone and source, with clear progress bars and flexible display options. Home Decor has so much to offer for anyone who is serious about building, selling decor, or progressing endeavors in multiple neighborhoods.

---

### [Housing Decor Guide](https://www.curseforge.com/wow/addons/housing-decor-guide) by *Vamoose*

![Housing Decor Guide](https://u-wowretail.raiderio.net/9bcd/2026/03/09/shseYfYuV9oIBJTuay1Xm4MorY/image.png)

Vamoose’s Housing Decor Guide offers everything you could possibly need for the housing endgame. Seriously, I mean that. This addon is **expansive**. Decor sellers will love the Auctionator and TSM integration and the Profit Analysis tab, which helps find the most profitable crafts and includes implied lumber value when crafting; the Vendor Shopping List offers multi-list support, waypoint integration, an import/export option, and more! In Zone Mode, Housing Decor Guide can alert you to nearby vendors, so you never find yourself wandering past a decor vendor again. The Acquire Tab is likely to be your most-used tab of all, allowing you to browse all the decor in the game by source, with incredible filtering options and an awesome “Map All” feature that will drop a waypoint on every vendor matching your current search filters. You can also preview every piece of decor via interactive 3D models. Vamoose also develops several other housing AddOns, so consider browsing their Curseforge profile if you enjoy Housing Decor Guide!

---

### [Housing Reps](https://www.curseforge.com/wow/addons/housing-reps) by *tr0tsky*

When early access player housing dropped with the Midnight pre-patch, a whole host of decor items were grandfathered in on old reputation vendors across Azeroth and beyond. Some intrepid explorers hopped on their mounts and started systematically visiting vendors right away, but for the rest of us, there’s Housing Reps. Not only does this AddOn track the new factions and rep systems first introduced in The War Within, but it also catalogs all reputation decor vendors as far back as Classic WoW. The rep tracking is account-wide, displaying your highest rep across all characters so you know which alt will be headed out on decor shopping trips. Right-clicking any faction heading will automatically set a map waypoint to the designated decor vendor, removing the need to Google-fu your way to finding each decor source from every expansion.

## Nameplates

### [Plater Nameplates](https://www.curseforge.com/wow/addons/plater-nameplates) by *Terciob*

![Plater Nameplates](https://cdnassets.raider.io/uploads/articles/659/jundies_plater.jpg)

*Plater with [Jundies’ profile](https://wago.io/ak3iS95aa)*

Plater is currently the most popular nameplate AddOn in the game, and sets the gold standard for this type of customization. The vast majority of popular WoW content creators, from RWF players to Mythic+ extraordinaires, use a Plater profile to keep their nameplates organized and color-coded to their specific requirements. Plater’s options are nearly endless, providing for a purely individual, tailor-made experience.

Plater can certainly seem daunting at first glance, but if you’re looking to get started, here are some options we recommend:

- [Jundies M+ and Raid](https://wago.io/ak3iS95aa) by *Jundies* is a plug-and-play profile with a support Discord available to troubleshoot any difficulties you may encounter while importing.
- [Whiiskeyz Plater](https://wago.io/whiiskeyzplater) by *Whiiskeyz* has been painstakingly customized for both high-level Mythic+ and raiding gameplay and is also available as a fully-fledged import with little need to modify or add to the profile provided.

---

### [BetterBlizzPlates](https://www.curseforge.com/wow/addons/betterblizzplates) by *Bodify*

BetterBlizzPlates does exactly what it says on the tin: it takes the base Blizzard nameplates and makes them incredibly customizable and adjustable. The AddOn comes with a set of pre-set profiles, including a Starter Profile for those interested in a quick start option, but you can also choose to start entirely from scratch and customize every piece by yourself. Hide unwanted nameplates, filter and adjust auras, display shields and absorbs, adjust fonts and textures, and SO much more. If you’re pretty happy with the base Blizzard plates but are hoping for just a little bit extra, picking up BetterBlizzPlates and adjusting just a few settings to start may be an ideal solution for you.

---

### [Platynator](https://www.curseforge.com/wow/addons/platynator) by *plusmouse*

![Platynator](https://u-wowretail.raiderio.net/8b3a/2026/03/09/QdrRDHmiBUMMeAxvnytwDUXXM4/image.png)

Platynator is the other go-to nameplate AddOn for Midnight, as it is lightweight, extremely visual, easily customizable, and offers a number of texture and color options. Adjusting your nameplates with Platynator is a unique and almost fun experience with the Visual Nameplate Designer, which allows you to click around the nameplate element and make adjustments to each nameplate widget independently. If you find Plater a little intimidating, Platynator is an excellent first step into the world of custom nameplates!
